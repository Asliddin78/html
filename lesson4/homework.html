A charity shop (British English) , thrift shop or thrift store (American English Canadian English and , also includes for-
profit stores such as Savers) or opportunity shop or op-shop (Australian English and New Zealand English) is a retail
establishment run by a charitable organization to raise money. Charity shops are a type of social enterprise . They sell
mainly used goods such as clothing, books, music albums, shoes, DVDs, toys, and furniture donated by members of
the public, and are often staffed by volunteers. Because the items for sale were obtained for free, and business costs
are low, the items can be sold at competitive prices. After costs are paid, all remaining income from the sales is used
in accord with the organization's stated charitable purpose. Costs include purchase and/or depreciation of fixtures
(clothing racks, bookshelves, counters, etc.), operating costs (maintenance, municipal service fees, electricity, heat,
telephone, limited advertising) and the building lease or mortgage .
Some charity shops also offer for sale a limited amount of edibles such as soda drinks, water, ice cream, snacks, and
candy. When available, these items are usually near the stores' cashiers.
